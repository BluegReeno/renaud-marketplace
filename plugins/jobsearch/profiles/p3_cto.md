# P3 — CTO

**Definition**: His former role (Open Ocean). Technical leadership, scale-up, fundraising. 2 cells ONLY — CTO only makes sense at startup/scale-up stage.

## Valid cells: 2 company types ONLY

- P3×T1 — Industrial startup / deeptech (Relief — score 92, Overstory)
- P3×T5 — AI SaaS early-stage (MuseIA, Virtuoz, Ublo)

## CRITICAL: Do NOT use P3 for

- BET / engineering consultancies (T2) — not a credible CTO fit
- ESN / consulting (T3) — CTO role doesn't exist here
- Large industrial groups (T4) — CTO in a grand groupe is a completely different animal

## Narrative rules

**Open Ocean IS the proof**: P3 is the only profile where Open Ocean leads. The CTO story is exactly what happened: built a marine data platform from lab to industrial deployment (team of 12, €2M raised, exit by Artelia). That's the proof.

**Stack must be current**: Unlike many former CTOs, his stack is live today. BWC v2.2 = Next.js + FastAPI + Pydantic AI + Supabase pgvector, France-hosted, built solo at maximum velocity.

**T1 vs T5 distinction**:
- T1 (industrial startup): Emphasise the scaling experience, team building, fundraising. Open Ocean is the CTO-who-shipped-at-scale proof.
- T5 (early-stage SaaS): Emphasise solo-velocity (BWC built alone) + the scaling-experience combo. This is what a non-technical founder needs.

## Do NOT include "founder" in the CV title

Never: "Co-Founder & CTO" as the CV headline. The title must be just "CTO — ...". The founder context stays in the experience section.

## Key differentiators

- Has been exactly this CTO: start to exit
- Current stack: production GenAI, not slide-ware
- Knows investors, tech debt, hiring — can run the whole function
